What's on screen the moment you pull out your phone mid-walk: the three things that decide whether you need to do anything — Free cash vs floor, VIX posture, P1 alert count. Header is <~80px. Compact but readable.

Walking use case flow: Phone opens directly to Focus. P1 alerts at top — if zero, you close the app and keep walking. If there are P1s, each one is a single card with the decision context (roll price, strike, DTE). No drilling required to know if something's on fire.

Why this works on mobile specifically:

  • Sticky header persists across modes. Switch to Explore and the same Tier-1 strip stays — you never lose context.
  • Bottom tabs, not top tabs. Thumb-reachable while walking. 3 tabs, not 7.
  • Dropped from header on mobile: MTD progress bar (moved to footer of Focus feed). ⌘K replaced with a 🔍 search tap. Trend arrow on VIX dropped — level + band is enough at this width.
  • Explore is a launcher, not a tab. First row on Explore is Positions / Radar / Macro chip cards → tap to drill in. Matches how you actually use those views (as "I'm curious" destinations).

What's NOT here on purpose: Ticker quotes. Journal tab hidden behind Review (rare on mobile per your 1-in-5 estimate). Dollar P&L.
